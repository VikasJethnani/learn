Regulatory Roadmap

Every license, certification, and approval needed to legally import Juan Valdez freeze-dried coffee from Dubai to India

CEPA Alert: Coffee (HS 2101) is on India's exclusion list under the India-UAE CEPA. No preferential duty rates apply — you pay the standard 30% BCD regardless of importing from Dubai. The CEPA covers ~90% of tariff lines but explicitly excludes tea, coffee, dairy, sugar, and tobacco.

India-UAE CEPA & Coffee

The India-UAE Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (signed May 2022) eliminated or reduced duties on ~90% of tariff lines. However, India protected sensitive agricultural products by placing them on an Exclusion List of 1,157 tariff lines (9.72% of total).

Coffee's exclusion means:

  • No tariff reduction — not immediate, not phased over years
  • No preferential BCD rate — standard MFN 30% applies
  • Same duty whether importing from UAE, Colombia, Brazil, or anywhere else
  • Rules of Origin are moot — you can't claim preference regardless

Additionally: Even if coffee were covered, a product manufactured in Colombia and merely traded through Dubai would NOT qualify. CEPA requires either "wholly obtained" in UAE (for agricultural products) or 40% value addition on FOB basis. Simple trading/redistribution does not qualify, and anti-transshipment provisions are actively enforced.

FSSAI Labeling — The Make-or-Break Requirement

The #1 reason food imports get held or rejected at Indian ports

Rectifiable vs Non-Rectifiable Label Elements

Non-Rectifiable — MUST be on the original product label before shipping. Cannot be added via stickers:

  • Date of Manufacture / Packaging
  • Best Before / Expiry Date
  • Country of Origin
  • Lot / Batch Number

Rectifiable via Sticker — Can be applied at a customs-bonded warehouse in India before FSSAI inspection:

  • FSSAI Logo + License Number
  • Importer Name & Address
  • MRP (Maximum Retail Price)
  • Vegetarian Symbol (green dot)
  • Nutritional Information (if not on original)
  • Language translation (Hindi/English)

Tip: Negotiate with Juan Valdez MENA to print the non-rectifiable elements in English on their production line. This saves you from stickering costs and eliminates the #1 rejection risk.

IS 2791:2022 — Soluble Coffee Powder Standard

BIS standard governing quality parameters for all soluble coffee in India

IS 2791:2022 — What You Need to Know

This is the fourth revision of India's national standard for soluble coffee powder. Key parameters tested:

  • Moisture: Max 5% by mass
  • Caffeine: Min 2.5% on dry basis (for regular; lower for decaf)
  • Total Ash: Max limits defined
  • Water-Soluble Ash: Min thresholds
  • pH: Specified range
  • Solubility: Must dissolve completely in hot water
  • Heavy Metals: Lead ≤1.0 mg/kg, Arsenic ≤1.1 mg/kg
  • Ochratoxin A: ≤5 μg/kg
  • Pesticide Residues: Per FSSAI MRL schedule

Practical tip: Get a Certificate of Analysis (CoA) from an accredited lab in UAE/Colombia BEFORE shipping. If your pre-shipment CoA shows compliance, the FSSAI port lab test is very unlikely to fail. This is your insurance against costly port rejections.

Key Test Parameters

Moisture ≤5%, Caffeine ≥2.5% (dry basis), Total ash limits, Heavy metals (Pb ≤1.0 mg/kg), Ochratoxin A ≤5 μg/kg, Pesticide residues per FSSAI MRLs, Microbiological parameters
