The #1 Mental Model Shift: Electricity cannot be economically stored at scale. A barrel of crude you can't sell today sits in a tank. A megawatt-hour of power you can't deliver at 3pm ceases to exist. Every hour is a separate, perishable commodity.
Dimension Oil / Commodity Power
Time Granularity Monthly / daily positions. A March crude contract is one position. Hourly or sub-hourly. March has 744 separate positions (one per hour). Each is a distinct commodity.
Storability Oil sits in tanks, ships, or pipelines. Inventory is a strategic buffer. You can hold and wait. No practical storage. An unscheduled position creates an imbalance settled at penalty prices by the TSO. No holding.
Location Global benchmarks (Brent, WTI, Dubai). Basis differentials are small & slow-moving. Intensely locational. The price at Node A can be €50/MWh while Node B — 200 km away — is €5,000/MWh due to grid congestion.
Price Indices A handful of global benchmarks: Dated Brent, WTI, Henry Hub, JKM, Platts assessments. Thousands of locational indices: one per node/hub/zone, per hour, for both day-ahead and real-time. Each TSO publishes its own.
Delivery Tanker, pipeline, truck. Scheduling is operational but not the primary risk driver. Grid scheduling via the TSO. Day-ahead nomination is mandatory. Failure to schedule = imbalance penalty.
Settlement Invoice on volume × price. Monthly cycle. Clean, simple. Multi-layer: DA auction settlement + RT imbalance settlement + ancillary services + resettlements extending 90 days.
Weather Impact Moderate: hurricanes disrupt supply, seasons affect demand. Dominant: wind speed drives supply, temperature drives demand, solar irradiance sets generation. Weather IS the market.
Curves in Endur Forward curve with ~30 monthly pillars per year. Manageable data volume. Hourly Price Curves (HPCs) with 8,760 data points per year per location. Requires shape factors and curve construction.

Key Insights

  • Power positions are vectors, not scalars — 744+ hourly data points per month vs. 1
  • Non-storability means every open hourly position is an active exposure with no "wait and see"
  • Location matters as much as time — prices can differ 100x between nodes 200 km apart
  • Weather is the primary driver, not geopolitics or OPEC decisions

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • Position reports will look vastly more complex — learn to read hourly profiles, not just totals
  • Your Endur Ops Services will process 100x more data points per cycle
  • Settlement reconciliation is multi-pass (initial + resettlements up to 90 days)
  • You'll work closely with scheduling/operations teams — the boundary between trading and ops blurs

How Power Markets Work

Electricity markets are a chain: generation feeds the grid, the grid delivers to consumers, and markets coordinate the price signals at every stage. Here's how it all connects — focused on European markets where Shell operates.

⚡

EPEX SPOT

Dominant short-term exchange for Central Western Europe. Part of EEX Group.

  • Day-ahead & intraday markets
  • 17 countries including DE, FR, NL, BE, GB
  • 2024 volume: 868 TWh (654 DA + 215 intraday)
  • Gate closure: 12:00 CET for day-ahead
★

EEX (European Energy Exchange)

Leipzig-based derivatives exchange. Where longer-tenor products live.

  • Monthly, quarterly, yearly futures
  • Phelix = German power benchmark
  • Cal products tradeable up to 6 years forward
  • Also: EU ETS (carbon), gas, GoO futures
☂

Nord Pool

Leading exchange for Nordic & Baltic regions. 16 countries.

  • Day-ahead (Elspot) & intraday (Elbas)
  • Prices vary by bidding zone & hydrology
  • Nordic system price = unconstrained reference
  • Also operates N2EX (GB day-ahead)
ICE Endex — The Intercontinental Exchange's Amsterdam-based platform. Prominent for OTC-cleared power products and natural gas (TTF, NBP). Trayport (ICE-owned) is the broker screen connecting OTC energy traders across Europe — the dominant platform for bilateral power and gas trading.

The Day-Ahead Auction: SDAC

Intraday Continuous Trading (SIDC)

After the day-ahead auction clears (~12:55 CET), the intraday market opens. Unlike the blind auction, intraday is a continuous order book — trades execute as soon as a buy and sell order match. Gate closure is typically 5 minutes before delivery. Products exist for 15-min, 30-min, and 60-min intervals. SIDC uses a shared order book across participating zones — a German seller can match with a Dutch buyer if interconnector capacity is available.

Why intraday matters: If your wind farm generates 20% less than forecast, you are "short" on your day-ahead schedule. You must buy the deficit on intraday before real-time, or face the TSO's imbalance price. Intraday is the last chance to fix your position before the grid settles it for you — at a penalty.

Balancing Market & Reserves

1
FCR — Frequency Containment Reserve Seconds
▼

The first line of defense. Automatically activated within 30 seconds of a frequency deviation from 50.00 Hz. Symmetric (responds in both directions). Think of it as the grid's reflex — no human decision involved. Procured through daily or weekly auctions by TSOs.

Example: A 1,400 MW French nuclear unit trips unexpectedly. Grid frequency drops. FCR from generators across Continental Europe activates automatically within seconds to arrest the decline.

2
aFRR — Automatic Frequency Restoration ~5 min
▼

Secondary reserve. Automatically activated within 5 minutes. Brings frequency back toward 50 Hz after FCR has stabilized it. Coordinated cross-border via the PICASSO platform (Platform for the International Coordination of Automated Frequency Restoration and Stable System Operation).

3
mFRR — Manual Frequency Restoration ~15 min
▼

Tertiary reserve. Manually activated by TSOs within 15 minutes. Slower but provides larger volumes. Coordinated cross-border via the MARI platform (Manually Activated Reserves Initiative), operational since 2022. This is the "heavy artillery" of balancing.

4
Imbalance Settlement Financial
▼

Every market participant is connected to a Balance Responsible Party (BRP). The BRP is financially responsible for any difference between its registered schedule and actual metered volumes within a settlement period (15 minutes in DE/NL/BE, 30 minutes in GB).

Imbalance prices are designed to be economically painful — above market when the system is short, below market when long. This incentivizes active position management in intraday.

Key Insights

  • Power markets operate in three time horizons: Forward (months/years), Day-ahead (D-1 auction), Real-time (intraday + balancing)
  • EUPHEMIA clears the entire European day-ahead market simultaneously — the world's largest daily optimization
  • Intraday is your last chance to fix forecast errors before the TSO settles your imbalance at penalty prices
  • Imbalance prices are intentionally punishing — the market design forces BRPs to manage their positions

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • In Endur, you'll configure indices for both DA and RT prices — they're separate market data feeds
  • Settlement modules must handle multi-pass resettlement (initial + corrections up to 90 days)
  • Scheduling workflows (pMotion) align with these market timelines — DA submission by noon, intraday adjustments until 5 min before
  • Balancing products (FCR/aFRR/mFRR) may have their own deal types if Shell participates in reserve markets

Transmission System Operators (TSOs)

TSOs own and operate high-voltage transmission networks (typically 110 kV+) and are responsible for grid stability, balancing, and cross-border flows. They are regulated monopolies. ENTSO-E coordinates 41 TSOs from 34 countries.

France RTE (Réseau de Transport d'Electricité) Nuclear-dominated system (~70%). Complex balancing due to large baseload blocks.
Netherlands TenneT TSO B.V. Same parent as German TenneT. Major trading hub due to TTF gas & power convergence.

Interconnectors create trading opportunities: price spreads between zones = profit for traders who can flow power across the link.

Congestion

When interconnector capacity is insufficient to equalize prices, persistent price spreads emerge.

German day-ahead can be €50/MWh while a neighbouring zone is €40 because the cable is full. Congestion rent (price diff × capacity) goes to TSOs to fund new infrastructure.

In Endur: FTR deals hedge this basis risk.

Key Insights

  • Know your TSOs by name — you'll see them daily in scheduling interfaces and settlement statements
  • Germany having 4 TSOs creates unique complexity for power trading operations
  • UK is asynchronous from Continental Europe — no AC ties, only HVDC. This limits flexibility.
  • Interconnector congestion is the #1 reason why power prices differ between countries

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • Each TSO has different data formats, APIs, and settlement processes — integration work varies by country
  • Endur's pMotion must be configured for each TSO's specific nomination protocols
  • Cross-border trading P&L depends on interconnector availability — monitor ENTSO-E transparency platform
  • Engineers must understand bidding zones to correctly configure location-based indices in Endur

The merit order is the sequence in which power plants are dispatched, ranked from lowest to highest marginal cost. The market clears at the price of the last (most expensive) unit needed to satisfy demand — the marginal plant. All cheaper plants receive the same clearing price.

Negative Prices: When renewable output exceeds demand + export capacity, prices go negative. Nuclear plants pay to keep running (restart is costly). Wind operators with feed-in tariffs generate regardless. In Germany, negative prices occurred for thousands of hours in 2024. EPEX SPOT allows prices down to -€500/MWh.
Merit Order Effect of Renewables: When wind/solar generate heavily, they push gas plants out of the dispatch order. The clearing price drops dramatically. This is why a sunny, windy Sunday in Germany can see prices at €0-10/MWh, while a cold, calm winter evening can see €200+/MWh. The market has two increasingly common states: very cheap (VRE abundant) and very expensive (VRE absent, gas setting).

Key Insights

  • The marginal plant sets the price for ALL plants — cheap renewables get paid the same as the most expensive unit needed
  • Gas plants (CCGTs) are the most common price setter in European markets — making gas price the primary driver of power prices
  • Renewables earn declining "capture rates" as they cannibalise their own price — a key PPA pricing concern
  • Carbon cost (EU ETS) is effectively a surcharge on coal and gas — it changes the merit order by making coal less competitive

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • Understanding merit order is essential for: PPA valuation, spark spread trading, and explaining price moves to stakeholders
  • Negative prices affect settlement — your Endur settlement rules must handle negative values correctly
  • Shape factors in HPCs reflect the merit order: peak hours (high demand, gas setting) vs. off-peak (low demand, renewables/nuclear setting)
  • Engineers must understand: the power price is NOT the cost of generation — it's the cost of the marginal unit

Power prices are driven by a complex interplay of fuel costs, weather, carbon policy, and grid constraints. Unlike oil (where OPEC and geopolitics dominate), power prices are shaped by forces you can literally feel outside your window.

⛽

Gas Price (TTF)

The #1 driver. CCGTs are the marginal price setter in most European hours. When TTF gas rises, power prices follow almost immediately.

Price transmission Power Price ≈ Gas (TTF) / 0.50 + Carbon × 0.41
☁

Weather

Temperature drives demand (heating in winter, cooling in summer). Wind speed drives supply. Solar irradiance sets daytime generation. Cloud cover matters.

  • Dunkelflaute = cold, calm, dark winter. Prices spike.
  • Wind storm = excess supply. Prices crash / go negative.
⚖

Carbon Price (EU ETS)

EU Emissions Trading System. Every tonne of CO2 requires an EUA allowance. At €65/t, coal generation adds ~€55-65/MWh in carbon cost; gas adds ~€27/MWh.

High carbon prices structurally favour gas over coal (fuel switching) and make renewables more competitive.

⚛

Plant Outages

Nuclear outages (planned maintenance or surprise) remove large baseload blocks. France's 2022 nuclear corrosion crisis — 32 of 56 reactors offline — sent prices to record highs.

⇄

Interconnector Flows

Cross-border capacity determines whether cheap power from one zone can reach expensive neighbours. Congestion = price divergence between zones.

📈

Demand Patterns

Daily: low overnight, peak 8am-8pm. Weekly: lower on weekends. Seasonal: winter heating peak, summer cooling peak (southern Europe). Industrial load follows factory schedules.

Key Insights

  • Gas-power correlation is the single most important relationship to understand — it drives most price moves
  • Weather forecasts move power prices before weather happens — the market is forward-looking
  • Nuclear outages in France can affect prices across all of Continental Europe via interconnectors
  • Demand is more predictable than supply (renewables) — supply volatility is the primary challenge

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • When traders ask "why is the curve up today?" — check TTF gas, weather forecast, and nuclear availability first
  • Your Endur market data feeds must include gas (TTF), carbon (EUA), and power indices — they're inseparable
  • Spark spread monitoring (Power - Gas/Efficiency - Carbon) should be a standard dashboard for any power trading desk
  • Weather data integration is a real engineering requirement — some teams build feeds from meteorological services

Power Products & Trading

From standardized exchange products to bespoke PPAs — the instruments of power trading, how they work, and how they're structured in practice.

█

Baseload

Flat delivery of 1 MW for every hour of the delivery period — 24/7, including weekends and holidays.

  • Cal 26 Baseload Germany = 1 MW for all 8,760 hours of 2026
  • Traded on EEX as "Phelix Base Year Futures"
  • The most liquid forward product
  • Key benchmark for budgeting and long-dated hedging
▲

Peakload

Delivery during Monday-Friday, 08:00-20:00 (12 hours/day). Excludes weekends and public holidays.

  • Captures hours of highest demand
  • Traded as "Phelix Peak Year Futures" on EEX
  • Peak-to-base ratio reflects demand shape
  • Off-peak = all hours NOT in peak
☰

Shaped Products

Custom delivery profile matching a buyer's actual consumption pattern — a factory's two-shift schedule, a retailer's demand curve.

  • More expensive than baseload (shaping cost)
  • Trader/generator absorbs profile & balancing risk
  • Common in corporate supply contracts
  • In Endur: requires TSD table with hourly volumes

Tenor Products — The Forward Curve

Tenor Period Liquidity Use Case
Day Single day delivery Very high (day-ahead auction) Short-term optimization, balancing
Weekend Saturday-Sunday block Moderate Weekend load coverage
Week Monday to Sunday Moderate Short-term hedging, position adjustment
Month Calendar month (e.g., Feb-26) High Standard hedging horizon, customer pricing
Quarter Q1-Q4 (e.g., Q2-26 = Apr-Jun) High Intermediate hedging, up to 11 quarters forward on EEX
Cal (Year) Full calendar year (e.g., Cal 27) Very high Primary long-term benchmark. Up to Cal 31 on EEX. PPA pricing reference.
From Oil Perspective: Think of Cal products like Brent annual strip contracts, Month products like monthly Platts assessments, and Day products like your spot market. The key difference: power has on-peak/off-peak splits at every tenor level. A Cal 26 Baseload and Cal 26 Peak are separate, independently traded instruments.

Key Insights

  • Baseload and Peakload are the two primary building blocks — everything else is derived from combining them
  • Cal products are the most-watched reference — equivalent to annual Brent averages in oil
  • "Shaping cost" is a power-specific concept: the premium paid to convert flat generation into a consumption-matching profile
  • Off-peak is becoming increasingly important as renewables produce more during formerly "quiet" hours

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • In Endur, every product type (baseload/peak/shaped) needs its own deal template with the correct hour profile
  • Forward curve construction must handle the base/peak split — you can't just store one monthly price like oil
  • Cal product prices flow through to PPA valuations, customer contract pricing, and budget forecasts
  • Your position reports must distinguish base vs. peak exposure for risk management

Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) are long-term contracts (5-20 years) between a generator and a buyer. They are the backbone of renewable project financing — without a PPA, most wind and solar projects cannot secure bank debt. Shell Energy Europe is a major PPA originator.

⚡

Physical PPA

Generator physically delivers power to the offtaker or via a trader. Direct energy transfer.

  • Generator sells all output at agreed price
  • Buyer receives physical electrons (or equivalent via grid)
  • Requires scheduling and balancing arrangements
  • In Endur: Physical power deal with long tenor + nomination workflow
⇄

Virtual / Synthetic PPA

No physical delivery. A financial Contract for Difference (CfD). Generator sells to grid at spot; financial settlement on the difference.

  • Settlement: (Strike - Spot) × MWh
  • Popular with tech companies (Google, Microsoft) who can't receive physical power
  • In Endur: Financial swap with settlement against actual generation volumes
☆

Sleeved PPA

A retailer (the "sleeve") stands between generator and buyer, handling balancing and supply obligations.

  • Corporate gets a clean supply contract with renewable attributes
  • Generator gets creditworthy offtake
  • Sleeve earns a fee for managing complexity
  • Shell often acts as the sleeve (route-to-market)

Guarantees of Origin (GoOs) & REGOs

A GoO is a certificate representing 1 MWh of electricity generated from a renewable source. It certifies technology (wind, solar, hydro), country, and generation time. GoOs are the primary mechanism for corporate renewable energy claims in Europe. The UK equivalent is REGO (Renewable Energy Guarantee of Origin).

Market dynamics: GoO prices collapsed from ~€7/MWh in 2023 to below €0.30/MWh by end of 2024, driven by massive oversupply as European renewable capacity expanded. In 2024: 1,084 TWh issued, 884 TWh cancelled. EEX has launched GoO futures for standardized trading. Shell Energy Europe has dedicated environmental products desks covering GoOs, REGOs, EUAs, and voluntary carbon credits.

Key Insights

  • PPAs are the #1 revenue certainty mechanism for renewable projects — no PPA = no bank financing
  • Shell's "route-to-market" business = acting as creditworthy offtaker + balancing service for renewable generators
  • Virtual PPAs are financial instruments — they look like swaps in Endur, settled against actual generation volumes
  • GoO/REGO trading is a separate market from power — you can buy green certificates without buying the underlying electrons

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • PPA deal capture in Endur is the most complex power deal type — multi-year, variable volume, custom settlement formulas
  • Your team will likely build custom Ops Service scripts for PPA settlement (importing meter data, calculating CfD payments)
  • GoO tracking requires its own data model — generation certificates linked to specific generation assets and time periods
  • Shell's notable PPAs: 600 MW German solar (Hansainvest), 300 MW Spanish solar (Solaria), REFHYNE 2 hydrogen project

Spreads measure the economics of converting fuel to electricity — the power equivalent of oil's crack spread. If you understand crack spreads, these are intuitive.

⚡

Spark Spread

Gross margin of a gas-fired CCGT power plant.

Spark Spread Power Price - (Gas Price / Efficiency)

Example: Gas at €30/MWh, CCGT at 50% efficiency → gas cost = €60/MWh. Power at €80 → Spark spread = €20/MWh.

◆

Clean Spark Spread

Spark spread adjusted for carbon cost (EU ETS).

Clean Spark Spread Power - (Gas/Eff) - (0.41 × Carbon)

Gas emission factor ≈ 0.411 tCO2/MWh at 50% efficiency. At EUA = €65/t → carbon cost = €26.7/MWh.

●

Dark Spread / Clean Dark

Same concept for a coal-fired plant.

Clean Dark Spread Power - (Coal/Eff) - (0.9 × Carbon)

Coal plants at 35-40% efficiency. Emission factor ~0.9 tCO2/MWh. Carbon cost is ~2x higher than gas.

Oil Analogy: Crack Spread (Crude → Refined Products) is directly analogous to Spark Spread (Gas → Power). The economics are identical: input fuel cost vs. output product value. Clean Spark adds carbon cost, just as a "clean" crack would add environmental compliance costs.

Key Insights

  • When Clean Spark Spread > Clean Dark Spread, gas dispatch is favoured over coal — this is the "fuel switching" signal
  • At ~€25-30/t carbon, the merit order structurally shifts from coal-first to gas-first
  • Negative spark spreads mean it's unprofitable to run a gas plant — they should be shut down (if flexible enough)
  • Spark spread trading is a core strategy: buy gas, sell power, hedge the margin

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • Spread calculations are standard Endur deal types — cross-commodity swap templates
  • Your Connex feeds need gas (TTF), carbon (EUA), AND power prices to compute spreads
  • APM P&L reports should include spread decomposition: power component, gas component, carbon component
  • Traders will expect real-time spark spread monitors — a common engineering request

In oil, scheduling is operational housekeeping. In power, scheduling IS the primary operational activity. Every day, you must tell the grid exactly what you'll produce and consume, hour by hour. Get it wrong, and the TSO settles the difference at penalty prices.

Multi-Pass Resettlement: TSOs frequently restate settlement statements — meter corrections, market corrections, dispute resolution. Resettlements can arrive up to 90 days after initial settlement. Your Endur implementation must handle importing revised statements, re-running calculations, and generating credit/debit memos. This is one of the most implementation-intensive aspects of power Endur.

Key Insights

  • Scheduling is not optional — failure to nominate creates an imbalance settled at penalty prices
  • The timeline is relentless: day-ahead by noon, intraday until 5 min before, then TSO balances in real-time
  • Imbalance = (Scheduled - Actual) × (Imbalance Price - DA Price). It's always a loss.
  • Resettlement means your "final" settlement number isn't final for up to 3 months

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • pMotion in Endur runs daily, not monthly. Your ops team lives in the scheduling module.
  • Imbalance P&L is a unique power concept — build reports that show DA P&L vs. imbalance P&L separately
  • Your settlement Ops Services must be designed for multi-pass execution — idempotent, reconcilable, auditable
  • Engineers need to understand: settlement in power is fundamentally different from oil invoice settlement

Oil EquivalentNo direct analog. Oil deals don't have hourly volume profiles. This is fundamentally new.
Performance Note: A single shaped deal for Cal-2026 has 8,760 TSD rows. A book of 500 shaped deals = 4.38 million rows. This is why KWA Analytics recommends separate Ops Service packages for Power vs. Gas — the data volume per deal is orders of magnitude higher.
3

▼

Same structure as a gas or oil swap, but with power-specific pricing.

Oil EquivalentIdentical to a Brent/WTI swap in structure. The complexity is in the number of fixings (720 hourly vs. ~22 daily for oil)
4
PPA in Endur
▼

The most complex deal type. PPAs are multi-year, variable-volume contracts requiring custom configuration.

Physical PPALong-term physical deal with TSD volumes from generation forecast. Settlement rule: true-up against actual metered generation imported from EDM.
Virtual PPAFinancial swap where the floating leg references actual spot prices and the notional volume is actual generation. Settlement formula: (Strike - LMP) × Actual MWh
Data IntegrationMeter data from EDM (Energy Data Management) system loaded via Data Series module or XML over HTTPS (XOHS) webservices
Hedge AccountingIFRS 9 / FAS 133 designation. Effectiveness testing configured in APM — same framework as oil, different underlying.
Implementation complexity: PPA settlement in Endur typically requires custom JVS scripts for importing meter data, calculating CfD payments with TSD-level reset variables, and handling the payment formula model. Value Creed's approach uses the Data Series module for aggregated generation actuals.

▼

A locational basis swap between two nodes. Hedges the price differential between a source and sink location.

Oil EquivalentLoosely like a basis swap (WTI-Brent), but power basis can be extreme — hundreds of €/MWh vs. a few $/bbl for oil.

Spark Spread Options
▼

Option on the margin between power price and fuel cost.

Payoff max(0, Power_Price - Gas_Price × Heat_Rate - VOM_Cost)

In Endur: options deal with custom payoff formula. APM module handles valuation using Black-76 or similar. Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Vega) computed against the power forward curve.

Key Insights

  • The deal entry framework is the same — the complexity is in the hourly granularity and TSD tables
  • A single Cal-year shaped deal creates 8,760 rows — vs. 12 rows for an oil monthly deal
  • PPAs are the most implementation-intensive deal type — custom JVS scripts, EDM integration, complex settlement formulas
  • FTRs have no clean oil analog — they're a fundamentally new instrument to learn

  • Your JVS skills will be heavily used for PPA settlement customization and ISO data integration
  • Separate Power Ops Service packages from Gas to avoid memory and processing bottlenecks

P&L Attribution for Power

Price Change P&L

Same concept as oil: Delta P&L from forward curve moves. But computed across 8,760 hourly curve points × hourly position volumes.

Curve Roll P&L

As time passes, each hour's forward price converges to its settlement value. Power has more granular roll than oil — daily roll effects are visible.

Imbalance P&L

Unique to power. The difference between your scheduled position (settled at DA prices) and your actual position (settled at RT prices). No oil equivalent. Always a cost.

Key Insights

  • Power position reports have vastly more rows — learn to read hourly profiles, not just totals
  • Imbalance P&L is a new concept with no oil analog — it's always negative (a cost), and minimizing it is a key metric
  • Physical positions feed scheduling; financial positions feed P&L — they're tracked separately in many configurations
  • APM simulation time scales with hourly data volume — optimize bucket configurations for performance

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • Build position reports that show DA P&L, realized P&L, and imbalance P&L separately
  • On-peak vs. off-peak position split is essential for risk management — traders manage these independently
  • APM bucket configuration is a key performance lever — get this right early
  • A European power firm using Endur reduced manual reconciliations by 70% by centralizing imbalance exposure management

Leading & Mentoring in the Power Domain

Beyond technical knowledge — the leadership skills, engineering challenges, and team-building approaches specific to power trading technology teams.

As a team lead, you're the bridge between business stakeholders (traders, ops, risk) and your engineering team. You don't need to trade power, but you must be able to translate trader requests into technical requirements — and push back when requirements are ambiguous.

The 10 Concepts You Must Master First

1
Non-Storability of Power
▼

Why it matters for leadership: Every technical decision flows from this. When a trader says "hold the position," you must understand that power positions can't be held — they must be scheduled or sold. When an engineer suggests a "retry later" approach for a failed nomination, you must know that in power, "later" means imbalance penalties.

2
Hourly Granularity
▼

Why it matters for leadership: Every data model discussion, every performance issue, every storage calculation comes back to this. An engineer who designs a monthly-grain data model will build a system that fails. You must catch this in design reviews.

3
DA vs. RT Pricing
▼

Why it matters for leadership: When a stakeholder asks for "the power price," you must clarify: DA or RT? Hub or node? Which zone? Which hour? An ambiguous requirement here causes weeks of rework.

4
Merit Order & Price Formation
▼

Why it matters for leadership: When a trader says "the curve moved because of wind," you need to understand the causal chain: more wind → renewables push gas out of merit order → clearing price drops. Without this, you can't prioritize weather data integration work.

5
Balancing & Imbalance
▼

Why it matters for leadership: Imbalance cost is the number your ops team and traders watch daily. When they request a "real-time position dashboard," they mean: show me my scheduled position vs. my forecast actual, and compute my likely imbalance cost. 6
Spark Spread Economics
▼

Why it matters for leadership: The spark spread is the fundamental P&L metric for gas-to-power operations. ▼

Why it matters for leadership: Each TSO has different APIs, data formats, scheduling protocols, and settlement timelines. 9

▼

Why it matters for leadership: When your settlement team says "the numbers changed again," you must know this is normal — TSOs restate settlements up to 90 days. Your systems must be designed for this from day one. Retrofitting multi-pass settlement is painful and expensive.

Power trading technology has unique engineering challenges that don't exist (or are much smaller) in oil/commodity systems. Knowing these upfront helps you plan, staff, and architect correctly.

⚠

Data Volume at Hourly Scale

The #1 engineering challenge. Everything operates at hourly (or 15-min) granularity.

  • A single deal for one year = 8,760 TSD rows
  • 500 shaped deals = 4.38 million rows
  • APM simulation: 8,760 price lookups × N deals per location
  • Connex feeds: hourly price updates, not daily

Design for hourly scale from day one. Retrofitting monthly-grain systems is a rewrite.

⏰

Time Zone Complexity

European power trading spans CET/CEST, GMT, EET. Time zone transitions create data challenges.

  • Daylight saving: March has 23 hours, October has 25
  • TSOs report in local time; exchanges in CET; Endur in configured TZ
  • Hour numbering: HE (Hour Ending) vs. HB (Hour Beginning)
  • 15-minute MTUs since Oct 2025 multiply complexity by 4
🔌

Multi-TSO Integration

Each country's TSO has different APIs, data formats, and protocols.

  • Germany alone has 4 TSOs with 4 different portals
  • No standardized API — XML, CSV, REST, SOAP all in use
  • Settlement statement formats vary by TSO
  • Certification requirements for automated submission
♻

Real-Time Operations

The boundary between "trading system" and "operations system" blurs in power. Daily scheduling is a production-critical workflow.

  • Nomination submission has hard deadlines (noon CET for DA)
  • Intraday position adjustments until 5 min before delivery
  • System downtime during scheduling window = imbalance penalties
  • SLA: pMotion and price loading are Tier 1 critical
📊

Curve Construction

Building hourly forward curves from monthly liquid prices + shape factors is non-trivial.

  • Shape factors change as renewable penetration grows
  • Weekend vs. weekday patterns differ by season
  • Holiday calendars affect profiles (Christmas, Easter)
  • Cross-commodity correlation: gas-power-carbon triangle
🔧

Settlement Reconciliation

Multi-pass resettlement is an engineering problem, not just a business process.

  • Scripts must be idempotent (safe to re-run with new data)
  • Audit trail required for every version
  • Delta calculations between settlement versions
  • Reconciliation against external TSO statements

Key Insights

  • Data volume is the root cause of most power system performance issues — not algorithm complexity
  • Time zones are a landmine — every daylight saving transition will break something if not handled carefully
  • pMotion and price loading are Tier 1 critical systems — treat them like production payment systems
  • Multi-TSO integration is where most project timeline overruns happen — scope carefully

Impact on Your Day-to-Day

  • Staff your team with engineers who are comfortable with time-series data at scale
  • Build monitoring dashboards for price loading, scheduling submission, and settlement batch jobs
  • Plan for DST transitions explicitly — add them to your release calendar and testing plan
  • Budget 3-4x engineering time for multi-country vs. single-country integration

SDAC — Single Day-Ahead Coupling

SDAC is the pan-European mechanism that links 27 countries, 61 bidding zones, 30 TSOs, and 16 NEMOs (Nominated Electricity Market Operators) in a single daily auction.

The core algorithm is called EUPHEMIA (EU Pan-European Hybrid Electricity Market Integration Algorithm), developed by N-SIDE. It's a mixed-integer optimization model that maximizes total social welfare (consumer surplus + producer surplus + congestion rent) across all 24 hours simultaneously.

Key facts:

  • Runs once daily at ~12:00-12:55 CET
  • EUPHEMIA solves in approximately 12-17 minutes
  • Handles simple hourly bids AND complex block orders spanning multiple hours
  • Implicitly allocates cross-border transmission capacity — traders don't bid for it separately
  • Since October 2025: 15-minute Market Time Units (MTUs) across all borders

This is arguably the world's largest daily optimization problem — millions of bids across 61 zones, 24 hours, with network constraints.

Guarantees of Origin (GoOs) — Deep Dive

GoOs are governed by the Association of Issuing Bodies (AIB) and the European Energy Certificate System (EECS). Each certificate represents exactly 1 MWh and records:

  • Technology: Wind, solar PV, hydro, biomass, geothermal
  • Country: Where the MWh was generated
  • Time period: When it was generated
  • Plant details: Specific generating facility

Market dynamics (2024):

  • Prices collapsed from ~€7/MWh (2023) to below €0.30/MWh (end 2024)
  • Cause: massive oversupply as European renewable capacity expanded + French nuclear recovery
  • 2024 volumes: 1,084 TWh issued, 1,067 TWh transferred, 884 TWh cancelled
  • "Cancelled" = claimed by an end consumer for renewable energy reporting

The UK uses REGOs (Renewable Energy Guarantee of Origin) — a similar but separate certificate system post-Brexit.

Shell Energy Europe trades GoOs, REGOs, EUAs, and voluntary carbon credits through dedicated environmental products desks.
